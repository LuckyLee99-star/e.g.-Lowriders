About Us

  • Where is all began
In the mid-twentieth century in Southern California, colorful painted classic cars would cruise the boulevards. From Cadillac El Dorados to Chevy Impalas, these lowriders were rolling art installations with chrome rims and white wall tires, with the cars meant to be driven "low and slow" as they hugged the pavement. Set on hydraulics, the cars would be able to, in the flip of a switch, become lowriders one second and street-legal vehicles the next.

    Fun Facts

  • #Fact 1
The lowriders trunk isnt meant for normal storage. The trunk of the lowriders has powerful sound systems & the hydraulic system management & storage is here itself.
  • #Fact 2
The Lowrider can move upside-down, side-to-side, and front-back because of the incompressible hydraulic fluid attached to different cylinders. Lowriders catch fire very smoothly while hopping.
  • #Fact 3
Massive motors have been installed by the lowrider car owners giving them more recognition among the hot rod communities & high-performance tuners.
  • #Fact 4
Lowriders have very small wheels nearly of size 13 inches to 14 inches although the standard car size should be 17 to 19 inches.
  • #Fact 5
Lowriders can dance, hop & show off very well. The best hopping or jumping is evaluated based on the height bars they can touch. Most of them jump up to a height of 12 feet on 4 wheels while the lowriders generally jump on one axle.
